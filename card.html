Flamingo

The word ‘flamingo’ comes from the Spanish word ‘flamenco’ meaning fire, which refers to the bright pink or orange colour of the feathers.

  • scientific name: Phoenicopteridae.
  • Average height: 1.1-1.5m.
  • lifespan: about 30 years.
  • Habitat: They are found on several continents, in warm and wet areas.

There are four flamingo species are distributed throughout the Americas, including the Caribbean, and two species are native to Africa, Asia, and Europe. Flamingos usually stand on one leg while the other is tucked beneath their bodies.
